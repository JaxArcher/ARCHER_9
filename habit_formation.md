# ARCHER Health Knowledge Base - Habit Formation

## 1. The Habit Loop
- **Cue**: The trigger (Time, Location, Emotional State, Other People).
- **Craving**: The motivational force.
- **Response**: The actual habit performed.
- **Reward**: The satisfaction that reinforces the loop.

## 2. Identity-Based Habits
- Focus on *who* you want to become (e.g., "I am a person who doesn't miss workouts") rather than just the outcome (e.g., "I want to lose 5kg").
- Every action is a vote for the type of person you wish to be.

## 3. Friction Management
- **Reduce Friction**: Prepare workout clothes the night before. Keep a water bottle on the desk.
- **Increase Friction**: Move the TV remote to another room. Delete distracting apps during focus blocks.

## 4. Implementation Intentions
- Use "I will [BEHAVIOR] at [TIME] in [LOCATION]" formulas to reduce cognitive load on decision-making.

## 5. Habit Stacking
- Attach a new habit to an existing, established one.
- Example: "After I close my laptop for the day (Current Habit), I will immediately do 20 pushups (New Habit)."

## 6. The 2-Minute Rule
- When starting a new habit, it should take less than two minutes to do. Optimize for the start, then scale the intensity.
