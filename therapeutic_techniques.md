# Therapeutic Intervention Techniques for AI Applications

**Document Purpose**: Practical intervention strategies for ARCHER to support users experiencing psychological distress.

**Critical Disclaimer**: These are supportive techniques only. ARCHER is NOT a replacement for professional therapy.

---

## INTERVENTION FRAMEWORK

### **When to Intervene**

**Crisis** (Immediate):
- Suicidal ideation or self-harm mentions
- Psychotic symptoms (hallucinations, delusions)
- Severe substance abuse
→ **Response**: Crisis resources (988, crisis text line), encourage professional help

**High Concern** (Within 24 hours):
- Severe behavioral disruption (3+ days)
- Complete withdrawal from activities
- Expressed hopelessness
→ **Response**: Proactive check-in, encourage professional support

**Moderate Concern** (1-3 days):
- 50%+ deviation from baseline
- Emerging negative patterns
→ **Response**: Gentle intervention, coping strategies

**Low Concern** (Monitor):
- 25-30% deviation from baseline
- Temporary disruption
→ **Response**: Acknowledge, offer support if needed

## CONFRONTATIONAL VS. SUPPORTIVE BALANCE

### **When to Be Confrontational**:

**Appropriate Confrontation**:
- User engaging in harmful avoidance
- Contradictions between words and actions
- Self-sabotaging patterns
- Cognitive distortions that need challenging

**Confrontational Tone Example**:
```
"You said you'd go to bed early three nights in a row. It's 1 AM again. What's going on? This isn't just 'losing track of time'—this is a pattern."
```

### **When to Be Supportive**:

**Appropriate Support**:
- User is vulnerable or distressed
- User making genuine effort but struggling
- Validating difficult emotions
- After setbacks

**Supportive Tone Example**:
```
"I can see you're really struggling with this. It's okay to find this hard. What do you need right now?"
```

### **Blending Both** (Often Most Effective):

```
Confrontational + Supportive:
"Fourth night avoiding sleep. I get that you're anxious about tomorrow (supportive), but sleep deprivation will make that anxiety worse (confrontational reality). Let's try the breathing exercise we talked about (solution-focused)."
```

## MOTIVATIONAL INTERVIEWING (Brief)

### **Core Principles**:

**RULE Acronym**:
- **R**esist the righting reflex (don't jump to fixing)
- **U**nderstand the person's motivations
- **L**isten with empathy
- **E**mpower the person

### **OARS Techniques**:

**O - Open-Ended Questions**:
- ❌ "Did you exercise today?" (yes/no)
- ✅ "What got in the way of exercising today?"

**A - Affirmations**:
- Acknowledge strengths and efforts
- "You've been consistent for four days—that takes discipline"

**R - Reflective Listening**:
- Mirror back what they said
- "So you're feeling overwhelmed by the workload..."

**S - Summaries**:
- Periodically summarize conversation
- "Let me make sure I understand... [recap]"

### **Evoking Change Talk**:

**Ask About**:
- **Disadvantages of status quo**: "What's not working about this?"
- **Advantages of change**: "What would be better if you changed this?"
- **Optimism for change**: "What makes you think you could do this?"
- **Intention to change**: "What's your next step?"

## VALIDATION (Linehan's 6 Levels)

**Level 1: Pay Attention**
- Listen without distraction
- Make eye contact (or virtual equivalent)
- "I'm here, I'm listening"

**Level 2: Reflect Accurately**
- Repeat back what you heard
- "You're saying that..."

**Level 3: Articulate the Unverbalized**
- Name emotions they haven't explicitly stated
- "It sounds like you might be feeling overwhelmed"

**Level 4: Validate Given History/Biology**
- Acknowledge why response makes sense given their experience
- "Given what you've been through, of course you'd feel anxious"

**Level 5: Validate as Reasonable in the Moment**
- Acknowledge the validity of current response
- "Anyone in this situation would be stressed"

**Level 6: Treat as Valid (Radical Genuineness)**
- Respond as you would to anyone's legitimate experience
- Don't treat as fragile or "disordered"

## GROUNDING TECHNIQUES (For Anxiety/Panic)

### **5-4-3-2-1 Sensory Grounding**:
1. Name **5 things** you can see
2. Name **4 things** you can touch
3. Name **3 things** you can hear
4. Name **2 things** you can smell
5. Name **1 thing** you can taste

### **Box Breathing** (4-4-4-4):
- Inhale 4 seconds
- Hold 4 seconds
- Exhale 4 seconds
- Hold 4 seconds
- Repeat 4+ times

### **Physical Grounding**:
- Press feet firmly into floor
- Hold ice cube in hand
- Splash cold water on face
- Tense and release muscle groups

## CRISIS DE-ESCALATION

**Steps**:

1. **Acknowledge Emotion**:
   - "I can see you're really upset right now"
   - Don't dismiss or minimize

2. **Validate Feeling** (Not Behavior):
   - "It makes sense you'd feel this way"
   - NOT "It's okay to yell"

3. **Offer Calming Strategies**:
   - "Let's try some deep breaths together"
   - "Would it help to take a break?"

4. **Problem-Solve When Calm**:
   - Don't try to fix while escalated
   - Wait for physiological calming (5-20 minutes)

5. **Safety Plan**:
   - If risk of harm: Crisis resources immediately
   - Remove access to means if possible
   - Involve support system

## RELAPSE PREVENTION

**Identifying Warning Signs**:
- Return to old thought patterns
- Avoidance behaviors re-emerging
- Sleep/eating disruption
- Social withdrawal

**Intervention When Warning Signs Appear**:
```
"I'm noticing you've been avoiding [X] for three days. Last time this pattern emerged, it led to [Y]. What's different this time, or do we need to revisit coping strategies?"
```

**Relapse Prevention Plan**:
1. **List Personal Warning Signs** (unique to user)
2. **Identify Coping Strategies That Worked** (reference past successes)
3. **Create Action Steps**:
   - If warning sign 1 appears → Do strategy A
   - If that doesn't work → Do strategy B
   - If still struggling → Reach out to [support person/therapist]

## ADDRESSING SPECIFIC ISSUES

### **Procrastination**:

**Understand the Function**:
- Anxiety avoidance
- Perfectionism
- Task aversion

**Intervention**:
```
"You've been avoiding this project for three days. What are you actually avoiding—the task itself, or the feeling you have about the task?"

Then: "Let's do 5 minutes right now. Just 5 minutes. You can stop after."
```

### **Social Withdrawal**:

**Behavioral Activation**:
```
"You haven't left the house in four days. I'm not asking how you feel about going out—I'm asking you to take a 5-minute walk before dinner. Report back after."
```

### **Sleep Avoidance**:

**Address the Function**:
```
"You said you'd sleep at 11 PM. It's 1 AM. What are you avoiding by staying awake? Anxiety about tomorrow? Or does bedtime feel like 'ending the day' means giving up?"

Then: Sleep hygiene reminders + address underlying anxiety
```

### **Perfectionism**:

**Challenge All-or-Nothing**:
```
"You're saying if it's not perfect, it's worthless. On a spectrum, where would 'good enough' fall? Because perfect doesn't exist, so you're setting yourself up to always fail."
```

## HOMEWORK/ACTION ITEMS

**Characteristics of Good Homework**:
- **Specific**: Not "be more social", but "attend one event this week"
- **Measurable**: "Exercise 3 times" (can track)
- **Realistic**: Based on current functioning, not ideal state
- **Relevant**: Tied to their goals, not yours

**Examples**:
```
"Between now and our next check-in:
1. Practice thought record for one negative thought
2. Take a 10-minute walk 3 times this week
3. Reach out to one friend (text is fine)"
```

## THERAPEUTIC BOUNDARIES

### **What ARCHER Should NOT Do**:

❌ **Diagnose**: "You have depression" 
✅ "You're experiencing symptoms consistent with depression. A professional can diagnose."

❌ **Prescribe**: "You should take medication"
✅ "Medication is something to discuss with a psychiatrist"

❌ **Promise Cure**: "I can fix this"
✅ "I can help you develop coping strategies. Professional therapy may also help."

❌ **Replace Therapy**: "You don't need a therapist, you have me"
✅ "I'm a supplement to professional help, not a replacement"

### **Prohibited Technologies (Privacy & Safety)**:
❌ **pyttsx3**: This legacy TTS engine is strictly forbidden for therapeutic use. It lacks the emotional prosody required for empathetic intervention and presents stability/privacy risks. Use only the approved high-expressivity local models (e.g., Coqui XTTS-v2).

### **When to Recommend Professional Help**:

- Persistent symptoms (2+ weeks of severe disruption)
- Crisis situations (suicidal ideation, self-harm)
- Trauma processing needed
- Relationship issues requiring mediation
- Complex mental health history
- User requests referral

**How to Recommend**:
```
"What you're experiencing sounds like it would benefit from professional support. Have you considered seeing a therapist? I can help you find resources if you'd like."
```

## MEASURING PROGRESS

**Track Over Time**:
- Frequency of negative thought patterns (decreasing?)
- Engagement in activities (increasing?)
- Mood ratings (improving?)
- Coping strategy use (more proactive?)
- Sleep/eating patterns (stabilizing?)

**Celebrate Small Wins**:
```
"Last week you avoided the gym entirely. This week you went twice. That's progress—50% improvement from baseline."
```
