# ARCHER Health Knowledge Base - Exercise Physiology

## 1. Core Principles of Adaptation
- **Overload Principle**: The body must be stressed beyond its current capacity to trigger adaptation.
- **Specificity**: Physiological responses are specific to the type of training (e.g., aerobic vs. anaerobic).
- **Recovery**: High-intensity exertion must be balanced with sufficient parasympathetic rest.

## 2. Metabolic Health Indicators
- **VO2 Max**: Maximum rate of oxygen consumption. A key predictor of cardiovascular longevity.
- **Lactate Threshold**: The point at which lactate enters the blood faster than it can be cleared.
- **RHR (Resting Heart Rate)**: Normal range 60-100 bpm. High RHR may indicate overtraining or chronic stress.
- **HRV (Heart Rate Variability)**: High HRV indicates good recovery and autonomous nervous system balance.

## 3. Strength and Hypertrophy
- **Mechanical Tension**: The primary driver of muscle growth.
- **Metabolic Stress**: "The Pump"—accumulation of metabolites like lactate.
- **Muscle Damage**: Micro-tears requiring protein-synthesis-led repair.

## 4. Sedentary Risks
- **Metabolic Suppression**: Sitting for >2 hours reduces lipoprotein lipase activity by up to 90%.
- **Postural Degradation**: Anterior pelvic tilt and kyphosis (hunched back) from prolonged laptop use.
- **Cognitive Decay**: Reduced blood flow to the brain decreases executive function.

## 5. Optimal Benchmarks
- **Steps**: 8,000+ daily for metabolic maintenance.
- **Resilience**: Minimum 150 minutes of Zone 2 cardio per week.
- **Strength**: Resistance training 3x/week focusing on compound movements (Squat, Deadlift, Press).
