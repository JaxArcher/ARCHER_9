# ARCHER Health Knowledge Base - Nutrition Science

## 1. Macronutrient Fundamentals
- **Protein**: Required for tissue repair and enzyme production. Recommendation: 1.6g - 2.2g per kg of body weight for active individuals.
- **Carbohydrates**: Primary fuel for high-intensity glycolytic activity. Focus on complex starches and fibrous vegetables.
- **Fats**: Essential for hormone regulation and fat-soluble vitamin absorption (A, D, E, K).

## 2. Micronutrients and Performance
- **Magnesium**: Critical for muscle relaxation and sleep quality.
- **Sodium/Potassium**: The electrolyte pump required for nerve conduction and muscle contraction.
- **Vitamin D**: Key regulator of immune function and testosterone levels.

## 3. Hydration Protocols
- **Baseline**: 30-35ml of water per kg of body weight.
- **Exertion**: Additional 500ml per hour of intense sweat.
- **Indicators**: Thirst is a late-stage signal; urine color (pale straw) is a better metric.

## 4. Metabolic Optimization
- **Meal Timing**: Protein intake should be distributed throughout the day (every 3-5 hours).
- **Glycemic Control**: Minimize refined sugars to avoid insulin spikes and subsequent energy crashes.
- **Satiety**: High-fiber and high-protein intake reduces overeating cues.

## 5. Inflammatory Indicators
- High intake of ultra-processed foods (UPFs) correlates with systemic inflammation and reduced recovery speed.
- Omega-3 to Omega-6 ratio should be maintained via fatty fish or supplementation.
